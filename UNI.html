The Role Of a Gameplay Programmer
by Felix Antonelli
The job of a gameplay programmer, in general, is to write code that the player interacts with whilst playing the game. The scope of the job can vary between companies with some expecting the gameplay programmer to complete jobs too brief to hire a specialist to do, whereas, some consider the job to be for those with both excellent design and programming skills. Often the job requires you to be versatile and able to adapt quickly to new disciplines as the task may span many areas of game development (Powell, 2012). A gameplay programmer should also be able to work well with a design team to make their element fit with the feel and style of the game and respond to feedback about improvements or bugs found by the Quality Assurance team. Any code written should be optimized for performance and memory usage as required, should come with clear documentation, and adhere to the studios coding standards. Powell, T., 2012. Job Description

• Leading and managing a team of Gameplay Programmers
• Work with production, engineers, design and art team leads to create task estimates and schedules for the gameplay engineering team and track development efforts across the team
• Balance active code development with the management requirements and responsibilities of a discipline lead
• Ensure the delivery of practical and timely solutions by the gameplay engineering team that meets the game design and technical requirements
• Ensure code is modular, self-contained, and defensive and observes the technical constraints on the system by leading regular technical design and code reviews.
• Ensure coding standards are followed, and help to enforce good programming practices within the engineering team
• Ensure the creation of technical documentation for the engineering team
• Mentor, manage and regularly review individuals to build a cohesive and effective team.
• Incentive and coordinate knowledge sharing within the team through trainings/talks.
• Optimize code and data for memory usage and performance as required.

Salaries
Glass door says the average pay for a Gameplay programmer can range from £22k/year to £48k/year.

Junior Gameplay programmers should expect anywhere from £16k/year to £27k/year.

Senior Gameplay programmers should expect anywhere from £35k/year to £62k/year.
Advice
Common advice for new Gameplay programmers is to keep asking questions, showing that you are willing to learn and adapt will keep your employer happy. If you have quite a few questions, write them down and ask them in one go rather than disturbing your boss multiple times a day. Another piece of advice is that large code bases can be daunting but you will get used to it, it just take time.
